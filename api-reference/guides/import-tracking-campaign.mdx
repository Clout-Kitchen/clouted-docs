---
title: 'Import content into a tracking campaign'
description: 'Attach a batch of content URLs to a tracking campaign and confirm they were ingested.'
---

**Outcome:** attach a batch of content URLs to the right tracking campaign and confirm they were
ingested.

<Note>
This is for **tracking campaigns** (`POST /tracking-campaigns/{uuid}/content`). For clipping
campaigns, use [Import clips into a campaign](/api-reference/guides/import-campaign) — a different
endpoint with a different `status` enum.
</Note>

<Steps>
<Step title="Find the tracking campaign uuid">
```http
GET /tracking-campaigns?pageSize=20
Authorization: Bearer <YOUR_TOKEN>
```

Read `data[].uuid` for the tracking campaign whose `name` matches.
</Step>

<Step title="Import the content URLs">
`POST` the URLs to that tracking campaign. Both `urls` (1–100 URIs) and `status` are required.

```http
POST /tracking-campaigns/{uuid}/content
Authorization: Bearer <YOUR_TOKEN>
Content-Type: application/json

{
  "urls": ["https://instagram.com/reel/abc"],
  "status": "pending"
}
```

`status` must be one of `pending`, `approved`, `rejected`.
</Step>

<Step title="Confirm ingestion">
List the tracking campaign's clips to verify they landed.

```http
GET /tracking-campaigns/{uuid}/clips?status=pending&pageSize=50
Authorization: Bearer <YOUR_TOKEN>
```

You can also filter by `platform`, `creatorUuid`, or `clipUrl`.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
Imports are capped at 100 URLs per call. Chunk larger sets and repeat the `POST`. A duplicate
submission or an unsupported-platform URL fails the request.
</Info>
